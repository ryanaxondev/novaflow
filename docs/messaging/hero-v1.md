# Purpose

This document translates the approved product strategy into implementation-ready messaging for the homepage Hero section.

Its purpose is to define what the Hero communicates before visual design and engineering implementation begin.

It introduces no new product decisions.

---

# Messaging Question

> What should the Hero communicate so that a first-time visitor immediately understands NovaFlow's value and knows what to do next?

---

# Guiding Principle

Messaging should communicate approved product decisions clearly rather than introduce new positioning. Clarity takes precedence over creativity.

Every headline, supporting sentence, and call to action should reinforce the approved product strategy while remaining concise, understandable, and conversion-oriented.

When messaging requires revisiting an approved product decision, the Product Specification—not this document—must be updated first.

---

# Messaging Boundaries

This document defines the communication layer of the Hero.

It determines what the interface says, not how it looks or how it is implemented.

It serves as the bridge between Product Strategy and User Interface Design.

# Core Product Message

Growing companies scale through repeatable operational systems—not by adding more task-management tools.

NovaFlow helps growing teams build repeatable operational systems that make collaboration more consistent, scalable, and reliable.

Rather than helping teams manage more tasks, NovaFlow helps them improve how work happens.

---

# Communication Goals

After reading the Hero, a first-time visitor should immediately understand:

- What NovaFlow is.
- Who NovaFlow is built for.
- What business problem it solves.
- Why NovaFlow is different from traditional work-management tools.
- Why repeatable operational systems matter.
- What action to take next.

The Hero should establish clarity before introducing features, pricing, or supporting evidence.

# Success Criteria

The Hero messaging is successful when a first-time visitor can understand, within a few seconds:

- What NovaFlow is.
- Who it is designed for.
- What business problem it solves.
- Why its approach is different.
- What action to take next.

If these questions cannot be answered after reading the Hero, the messaging should be revised before design or implementation continues.

The messaging should communicate value before asking for action.
